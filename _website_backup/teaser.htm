The Pursuit

Zora is the Slavic word for dawn: that specific shade of possibility between absolute darkness and the moment the sun commits to rising. Finding Zora is a game built around chasing it.

Every expedition is a scored attempt at the perfect sunrise. The scoring system has a name: the Eos Index, named for the Greek goddess of dawn, with a leaderboard that accumulates across the entire series. The player levels up like a D&D character. Each level unlocks a new piece of equipment. The game ends, theoretically, when a perfect sunrise is found. That score is 100. It hasn't happened yet.

"Every dawn is a scored event. Most people sleep through the leaderboard."
The Two Pillars

Every expedition produces two numbers.

100
Eos Index · max score
The pure quality of the sunrise. Sky, setting, and conditions. What the dawn gave you.
~215
Zora Score · theoretical max
The full expedition score. Travel, discovery, elevation, and adversity stack on top of the Eos Index. What you earned.
The Master Formula
Zora Score = Eos Index + Discovery + Travel + Conditions Theoretical maximum: ~215 pts · A perfect Eos Index of 100 is the game's ultimate objective
How Scoring Works

The Eos Index breaks into three components. The sky is the dominant factor, but where you stood to watch it, and how hard it was to get there, matter too.

Sky · 50 points maximum
Color Intensity/ 20 pts
Cloud Engagement/ 15 pts
Horizon Definition/ 15 pts
Setting · 30 points maximum
Foreground Composition/ 15 pts
Location Uniqueness/ 15 pts
Conditions · 20 points maximum
Effort to Reach Position/ 10 pts
Environmental Challenge/ 10 pts

On top of the Eos Index, the Zora Score adds points for how far you traveled, how rough the road was, how much elevation you climbed, how early you arrived, and what you found along the way.

The Core Principle

A perfect sky witnessed from a parking lot will always score lower than a modest sky earned at the top of a difficult pre-dawn ascent. The Eos Index measures the sky. The Zora Score measures the player.

The Discovery Log

Every species, geological formation, and cultural site encountered across the series is logged in the Discovery Log. First encounters are Unlocks: they earn points and they're permanent. The log accumulates over the life of the series and becomes its own documentary record.

Discoveries are tiered by rarity. Rarity determines points:

Leveling Up

The player advances through ten levels by accumulating Zora Score points across all expeditions. Each level unlocks a specific piece of equipment, building the production rig one earned upgrade at a time. The drone doesn't appear until Level 4. That's intentional.

The Record

Before Season One began, eleven existing photos were scored to calibrate the system. The current Eos Index record is 85 points: an ocean cliff silhouette, earned on a coastal hike at a latitude the desert saguaros have never visited. Every episode of Finding Zora is a chase for that number, and beyond.

Home base is the Sonoran Desert of Arizona. But Zora does not belong to any single landscape. She has been spotted over the Atlantic, above the treeline in the Rockies, rising from the sea off coastal cliffs. Wherever dawn breaks, the game is on.
