## What to say on camera

This is the pilot. The audience learns the rules here. You're teaching, not performing.

### Before sunrise — the intro

> "This is expedition one. Every sunrise gets scored — and this is where we set the benchmark."

Key beats to hit:
- This is The Zora Files — you're chasing sunrises and scoring them
- Explain what the Eos Index is: a 0–100 sunrise quality score
  - Sky (50 pts): color intensity, cloud engagement, horizon definition
  - Setting (30 pts): foreground composition, location uniqueness
  - Conditions (20 pts): effort to reach, weather/environmental challenge
- Mention the Zora Score builds on top — adds travel, discoveries, effort
- "Every episode competes against every other episode. This one sets the floor."

### During sunrise — narrate what you see

Talk through the scoring categories as they happen:
- **Color intensity** — "Look at that band of orange above the ridge — that's what we're scoring"
- **Cloud engagement** — clouds lit from below are gold; clear sky scores lower here
- **Horizon definition** — sun rises BEHIND the Superstitions here, so glow above ridgeline, not disk emergence. Call this out — it affects the score
- **Foreground** — the saguaros, the rock formations, whatever's in frame
- **Location uniqueness** — Superstition Mountains are iconic, lean into it

Don't force it. Pause. Let the sunrise breathe. The tension between your system and nature's unpredictability IS the show.

### After sunrise — the score

> "Alright, let's score it."

- Walk through each Eos Index category with your rough numbers
- Add up the Eos Index total
- Then layer on the Zora Score components: travel, effort, discoveries
- Announce both scores
- "That's the benchmark. Every expedition from here on competes against this."

### Discovery log intro

When you spot wildlife:
- "First unlock — [species name]. That goes in the Discovery Log."
- Explain: first time you find a species = first unlock, worth bonus points
- Every subsequent sighting of the same species = fewer points
- You're building a living catalog of everything encountered across all expeditions

### Level-up system (brief mention)

- "Six expeditions to level up. Score doesn't matter for leveling — it's about showing up."
- "After six, I earn the first medallion. After sixty, if I get there... that's Finding Zora."
- Keep it brief — plant the seed, don't over-explain in episode one
