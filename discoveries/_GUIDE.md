# Discoveries — guide

This folder tracks the Discovery Log — every species, geographic feature, cultural site, and historical find across all episodes worldwide.

## Files

- **discovery-log.md** — The master list of all discoveries, updated after each episode
- Individual species/feature write-ups can be added as needed for website content

## Point values by rarity tier

### First unlock (full points — one time only)
| Tier | Examples | Points |
|------|---------|--------|
| Common | Gambel's Quail, Cactus Wren, Cottontail | 5-10 |
| Uncommon | Gila Woodpecker, Rock Wren, Roadrunner | 15-20 |
| Rare | Vermilion Flycatcher, Osprey, Javelina | 25-35 |
| Very rare | Bald Eagle, Desert Tortoise, Chuckwalla | 40-50 |
| Exceptional | Once-per-series finds | 60-75 |

### Subsequent finds
- **Common/Uncommon:** 0 points on repeat (no repeat scoring)
- **Rare and above:** 1 point each subsequent find (separate outings only)

## Discovery types

- **Wildlife** — birds, mammals, reptiles, insects
- **Plant** — notable or rare species
- **Geographic** — formations, water bodies, elevation records
- **Cultural/Historical** — petroglyphs, ruins, landmarks, mining history, abandoned structures

### Human History Discovery (dedicated subcategory)
| Significance | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| Minor historical marker | 5 |
| Notable site (ruins, mining camp) | 10 |
| Significant site (petroglyphs, ceremonial) | 15-20 |

## How discoveries feed into scoring

Discovery points contribute directly to the **Zora Score** (the full episode score). They do NOT affect the Eos Index (which is purely sunrise quality). This means a mediocre sunrise at a discovery-rich location still earns a respectable Zora Score.

## How entries feed the website

Each discovery becomes a card on the Discovery Log page showing: name, episode found, point value, photo, fun fact, and location/country. The running total displays on the home page. Discoveries from any location worldwide are welcome — the system is not limited to Arizona or the desert Southwest.
